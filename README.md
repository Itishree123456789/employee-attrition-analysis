
# Employee Attrition Analysis

## Project Overview

Employee attrition refers to employees leaving an organization over a period of time. High attrition can increase recruitment costs, reduce productivity, and result in the loss of experienced talent. This project analyzes employee data to understand attrition patterns and identify factors that may influence employee turnover.

---

## Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze employee data and identify factors associated with employee attrition. The analysis focuses on understanding how department, age, salary, and job satisfaction relate to employee turnover and retention.

---

## Dataset

Dataset: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset

Columns Used:
- Age
- Department
- MonthlyIncome
- JobSatisfaction
- Attrition

---

## Key Insights

### Attrition Distribution
- Out of 1,470 employees, 237 employees left the organization while 1,233 employees remained.
- The overall attrition rate is relatively low, indicating strong employee retention.

### Attrition by Department
- Research & Development and Sales departments account for the majority of employee turnover.
- Human Resources has the lowest attrition count among all departments.

### Employee Age Distribution
- Most employees belong to the 30–40 age group.
- The workforce is primarily composed of mid-career professionals.

### Average Salary by Department
- Sales employees have the highest average monthly income.
- Salary levels vary across departments and may influence retention.

### Job Satisfaction vs Attrition
- Employees with lower job satisfaction show higher attrition.
- Higher job satisfaction is associated with better employee retention.

---

## Key Findings

- Employee retention is significantly higher than attrition.
- Research & Development and Sales departments experience the highest employee turnover.
- Most employees belong to the 30–40 age group.
- Salary levels differ across departments.
- Job satisfaction appears to influence employee retention.

---

## Conclusion

This project analyzed employee attrition patterns using workforce data. The findings indicate that factors such as department, salary, and job satisfaction may influence employee turnover. Understanding these patterns can help organizations develop effective retention strategies and improve workforce stability.
